A working manual · field notes on shipping

An AI-first design process, from fuzzy problem to shipped product.

I work AI-first. I use language models to compress the slow parts of design, understanding, synthesis, and exploration, so my time goes to judgment and craft. What follows is the five-stage method every project runs through, drawn as figures.

  1. 01

    Start with an LLM

    Understand before designing.

    I get fluent in the problem before I open a design tool, pressure-testing intent, synthesizing research, and mapping edge cases in minutes instead of days.

    • Clarify user intent
    • Synthesize research
    • Map edge cases
    • Sketch patterns

    OutcomeA sharp read on the user, the constraints, and where the real leverage is.

  2. 02

    Validate assumptions

    Talk to people before committing.

    I go to users early, through interviews, support tickets, and behavioral data, to replace opinions with evidence about what they're trying to do and where they get stuck.

    • Customer interviews
    • Usability sessions
    • Ticket mining
    • Behavioral data

    OutcomeEvidence replaces assumptions, so the riskiest ideas fail cheaply.

  3. 03

    Prototype with AI tools

    Design the behavior, not the screen.

    I build runnable prototypes so teams can feel the interaction, its states, transitions, and failure modes, and decide on behavior before anyone invests in polish.

    • Interaction design
    • User flows
    • State handling
    • Motion & feedback

    OutcomeDecisions get tested in something real, before significant build cost.

  4. 04

    Bring it into Figma

    Make it scalable and consistent.

    Validated concepts become systems: clear hierarchy, reusable components, accessible defaults, and specs engineering can ship without guesswork.

    • Visual hierarchy
    • Components
    • Accessibility
    • Production specs

    OutcomeA cohesive, scalable experience, refined in Figma, not invented there.

  5. 05

    Iterate with users

    Shipping is the start, not the finish.

    After launch I watch real behavior, analytics, session recordings, adoption, and friction, and feed what I learn straight back into the next round.

    • Usability testing
    • Product analytics
    • Session recordings
    • Friction analysis

    OutcomeContinuous improvement driven by behavior, not opinions.
